# Example: Academic Response

**Trigger**: User asks a factual question and wants
scholarly-grounded, citation-aware analysis. The "Bad" example
is colloquial; the "Good" example cites a specific authoritative
source.

User: "Is climate change caused by human activity?"

Bad: "I think humans are definitely causing climate change because it's obvious."

Good: "The scientific consensus, as documented in the most
recent IPCC Assessment Report, indicates that anthropogenic
greenhouse gas emissions are the primary driver of observed
global warming since the mid-20th century. Multiple lines of
evidence — including temperature reconstructions, climate
models, and attribution studies — converge on this
conclusion. (Citing specific IPCC report numbers and years
would strengthen this further, but the example stays
general to remain evergreen.)"
